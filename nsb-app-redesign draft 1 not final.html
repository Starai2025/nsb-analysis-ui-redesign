Executive Summary
Archer Western Construction, LLC requests modification of design document ownership from Work-Made-For-Hire assignment to a limited project-use license. This constitutes a material scope reduction. The original contract pricing reflected full IP transfer under Exhibit P Article 4.2 and Article 12.4. The request does not comply with Article 4.3 Claims procedures and lacks written PM authorization under Article 13. No time extension is warranted.
02
Owner / Client Request
Archer Western seeks to replace Work-Made-For-Hire IP assignment with a limited project-use license for design documents. This revises scope by retaining ownership with the subcontractor and granting only project-specific use rights. It does not change physical work or schedule but materially reduces the ownership interest conveyed in the contract deliverables.
03
Position Assessment

Likely No
Retaining IP rights while delivering only a license is a material scope reduction — should be priced as a deductive change. No time impact is anticipated because the physical work and schedule remain unchanged.
04
Key Contract Clauses
Article 4.1 ChangesPage 6
Contractor has sole authority to approve or reject modifications. Contractor may execute unilateral change orders binding on Subcontractor.
Why It Matters
Walsh Construction controls whether this request is negotiable. If rejected, the subcontractor must perform under the original Work-Made-For-Hire terms with no compensation adjustment.
Article 4.3 ClaimsPages 6–7
Initial Notice titled 'Initial Notice of Claim' required within 5 days of foreseeable impact. Full Claim within 15 days including contractual basis, cost/schedule impact, and certification.
Why It Matters
The current request does not comply with timing, titling, or content requirements. Non-compliance may waive any fee entitlement.
Exhibit P Article 4.2 & 12.4Pages 71–72
Work-Made-For-Hire: all documents, designs, and deliverables are the exclusive property of the Owner upon creation.
Why It Matters
Foundational contract term affecting pricing. Any reduction in IP transfer is a scope reduction and must be priced as a deductive change.
06
Commercial Analysis
Fee A fee adjustment is supportable in principle as a deductive change order. The subcontractor must quantify the value reduction between full IP transfer and a project-use license under Article 4.3 procedures. Pricing status: TBD.

Time No time extension supportable. Physical work, design schedule, and milestones remain unchanged. Schedule status: no extension warranted.
10
Recommendation
If Walsh Construction is willing to negotiate: direct Archer Western to submit a compliant Article 4.3 Claim within 15 days quantifying the deductive change value, supported by contractual basis and certified by a senior official. Process as a deductive change order and obtain written PM approval under Article 13.3 before execution.

If declining: issue written rejection notice, prepare unilateral change order under Article 4.1 to enforce original terms, and invoke Article 3.12 Set Off rights if deliverables are withheld.
